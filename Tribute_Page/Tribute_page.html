Nikola Tesla

  • Nikola Tesla (1856–1943) was a revolutionary inventor, engineer, and futurist.
  • Born in modern-day Croatia, he moved to the U.S. and worked briefly with Thomas Edison before parting ways due to differing views on electrical systems.
  • Tesla championed alternating current (AC) over Edison's direct current (DC), leading to the famous "War of Currents," which he ultimately won with the help of George Westinghouse.
  • Beyond AC power, Tesla invented the Tesla coil, helped advance wireless communication, and even conceptualized wireless energy transmission.
  • He dreamed of providing free electricity worldwide and designed the Wardenclyffe Tower, a massive wireless power station, but lost funding.
  • Later in life, he became more eccentric, focusing on projects like death rays and thought photography.
  • Though he died poor and overlooked, his contributions shaped modern technology, and today, he’s recognized as one of history’s greatest inventors.
